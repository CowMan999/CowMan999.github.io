About Me

Hardware instincts,
systems thinking.

I'm Edward Mayen, a first-year Computer Engineering student at McMaster University (Class of 2029), based in Mississauga, Ontario. I'm drawn to problems that live at the edge between circuits and code — where a firmware bug can literally set something on fire.

My hands-on experience spans embedded firmware, PCB design, robotics control systems, and systems-level programming. I've built everything from bare-metal OS kernels and 3D physics engines to custom sensor PCBs that survive race-vehicle vibration.

Beyond engineering, I compete nationally in computing and mathematics, produce music, and stay active through biking, track, badminton, and volleyball.
